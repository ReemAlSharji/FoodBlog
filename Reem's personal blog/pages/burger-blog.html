About recipe


A Swiss burger is a delicious and savory variation of the classic hamburger that incorporates Swiss cheese as a key ingredient. This type of burger typically features a high-quality beef patty, seasoned to perfection and cooked to the desired level of doneness. What sets the Swiss burger apart is the addition of Swiss cheese, a mild and nutty cheese known for its distinctive flavor and excellent melting qualities.
The Swiss cheese is often melted over the beef patty during the cooking process, creating a gooey and flavorful layer that enhances the overall taste and texture of the burger. This melted cheese adds a creamy and slightly sweet element to the savory beef, contributing to a well-balanced and satisfying flavor profile.
In addition to the Swiss cheese, a Swiss burger may be complemented with various toppings and condiments to enhance its overall appeal. Common additions include fresh lettuce, juicy tomato slices, pickles, onions, and a spread of mayonnaise or mustard. The choice of bun can also play a crucial role, with options ranging from classic sesame seed buns to artisanal choices like pretzel buns or ciabatta rolls.
The Swiss burger is often enjoyed for its rich and indulgent taste, making it a popular choice among burger enthusiasts. Whether served in a casual dining setting, at a barbecue, or in a gourmet restaurant, the Swiss burger offers a delightful twist on the traditional hamburger experience.

Ingredients

  • ground beef 80/20 or 90/10 mix
  • burger seasoning
  • Swiss cheese
  • brioche buns or buns of your choice
  • unsalted butter
  • white mushrooms
  • sweet yellow onion
  • salt
  • pepper

How to make swiss burger :

  1. Heat a grill or a cast-iron skillet on the stove over medium heat. Form ground beef into 4 evenly-sized patties. Season each patty with a good amount of burger seasoning.
  2. Cook a batch of saut√©ed mushrooms and onions and set aside. It takes just 10 minutes to make these mushrooms and onions, and they are delicious!
  3. Add patties to preheated grill or pan. Cook for 8 minutes on one side, then for 6-8 minutes on the opposite side, or until cooked through. Turn off the heat.
  4. Top with a slice of Swiss cheese and let it melt while on the grill or pan. Once melted, remove the burgers and place them each onto a brioche bun. Top with mushrooms and onions, and serve.
